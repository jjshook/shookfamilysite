Death Notice (Kenneth)

Kenneth N. Whitney, loving husband, father, grandfather, brother, uncle, cousin, and friend passed away on April 5, 2012, at the age of 70 of esophageal cancer.

Ken was preceded in death by his parents, Susan and Oliver Whitney, his brother, Tom Whitney and his niece Kara Beth Whitney.

Ken graduated from Sam Houston State University with a degree in history. He taught history and government at Galena Park High School where he also coached football and baseball. He loved his students and rejoiced when he could instill in them a love of our country's history. He was a loyal friend and a loyal fan of the Houston Astros, Texans and Rockets...win or lose. He loved crossword puzzles, Jeopardy, old movies, hunting, and sports of any kind.

Ken is survived by his wife of 44 years Sharon Whitney; sons Ken Whitney and wife Randi, Sean Whitney and fiance Doria Wright; grandchildren Jake, Luke, Lauren, and Lane Whitney; brother, Glenn Whitney and wife Martha; sisters-in-love Julia Whitney and Becky Henderson; numerous nieces and nephews and special friends.

The family would like to express their sincere appreciation to Dr. Heinz Hermann of Pasadena and Dr. Blum of M.D. Anderson for their compassionate care. The doctors, nurses and staff at M.D. Anderson were wonderful to Ken and our family throughout this journey.

The family received friends from 11:00 AM to 12:30 PM on Friday, April 13, 2012 in The Chapel of Grand View Funeral Home. Funeral services followed at 12:30 P.M. with interment at Grand View Memorial Park.

Notes

The 1910 census for the Whitney family is misleading. Olivia is indexed as the third child of Andrew Jackson Whitney and Artie Belle (Shook) Whitney as a female born in Texas. That is incorrect. Oliver Namon Whitney, born on 22-AUG-1907 in Texas, was the third child of Andrew and Artie. Their fourth child, Elvera Elizabeth Whitney, was born on 19-NOV-1910 in Louisiana (not Texas) several months after the 1910 census (enumerated on 23-MAR-1910). Hence, at the time of the enumeration, Andrew and Artie had three boys and no girls yet. The 1920 census is just as misleading. Elvera is indexed as "Elvia" and that can easily be interpreted as Olvia (and then extrapolated as Olivia to match the 1910 census).
