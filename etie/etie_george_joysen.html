Given Name

Geo. J. Etie's death certificate records him as the son of J. D. Etie and Ada Shook. David Roy Etie's birth certificate records George Joysen as his father. It's a good assumption that Geo. J. is short for George Joysen. While the 1920 census records Emanuel J. as the son following Jeff Davis, Jr. and preceeding Clyde Webster, I am assuming Emanuel was a nickname at that time.

Birth

The 1920 census and David Roy Etie's birth certificate both record George Joysen's birth in Texas. In contrast, the informant (Mrs. Gladys Etie) on George Joysen's death certificate records birthplace as Sulpher, Louisiana. I have not been able to determine who Gladys Etie is yet. In David Roy's delayed birth certificate (1943), Elva (Conrad) Etie Blackburn records George Joysen's birth in Texas. Given that Elva was married to George Joysen and Gladys Etie is an unknown variable, I have decided to record George Joysen's birth place as Texas (probably Harris County).
