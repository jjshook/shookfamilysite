History

Frederick Neidigk, Jr. was single when he died and had no children. He was a World War II veteran, had just come back from combat and gone into logging (the family business) when a few months later he was killed in an automobile accident not far from the Pate Cemetery. A logging truck ran into him and another Neidigk (per Jerry Vessels).
