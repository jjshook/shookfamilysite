Surname

Recorded documents show Johannes Georg "Hans" Schuck, der Junger (John George "Hans" Shook, Jr.) used the German surname Schuck. In contrast, his children switched to the English surname of Shook. Note that the suffix "der Junger" is equivalent to "Junior" in English.

Given Name

Johannes Georg is spelled his middle name with the 'e' left off (the German way) as Georg. In English, the 'e' is appended the way we are familiar with in the United States of America; that is, George. During immigration, his name is recorded as Hans (nickname) and George (the English way) in his father's will.

Immigration

Johannes Georg "Hans" Schuck arrived in the United States of America on 17-OCT-1732. He landed in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania on the ship "John & William" via Rotterdam with his family. He is recorded in the ship's registry as Hans Shooken.
