Obituary (Vivian)

Vivian, 85, passed away at the Mansfield Methodist Hospital.

She was born in Bridgeport, Texas, to John and Elsie Walker and moved to Odessa as a young girl.

She lost her first husband, Burtis Scarborough, in action with the U.S. Navy during World War II. She continued working to support the war effort and married Joseph W. Shook, Jr. in 1944, at Cape Canaveral, Florida.

After the war, they returned to West Texas and lived in a variety of locations throughout the Permian Basin before settling in Odessa in 1968. She completed High School in Denver City and later attended classes at Odessa College. Vivian participated in many civic organizations including: Eastern Star, Rebecca's, and Royal Neighbors.

She is survived by her son, Colonel John E. Shook, USMC RET and his wife, Linda; and grandchildren, John Jr. and Aubrie Rae; her daughter-in-law, Delisse Shook; grandson, Joseph W. Shook, IV and his wife, Dee; and great-grandchildren, Damion and Patrick; and grand-daughter, Michelle and her husband, Rex Chitty; and great-grandchildren, Ryan, Cory and Cody and countless nieces and nephews.

She was preceded in death by her husband of forty four years, Joseph W. Shook, Jr. (1989), her son, Joseph W. Shook, III. (2007), four brothers and two sisters.
