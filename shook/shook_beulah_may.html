Obituary

Mrs. Beulah May Zlomke, a Baytown resident for 16 years, died at 2 a.m. Saturday in her home at 1312 Yupon. Services are scheduled for ? p.m. Monday in Earthrnan Chapel. Sister L. A. Harvey, pastor of Revival Center Church, will officiate. Mrs. Zlomke will be buried in Crown Cemetery in Pasadena. The 76-year-old woman is survived by two sons, Frank Zlomke of Brazoria and Fred Zlomke of Baytown, and five daughters, Mrs. Viola Stewart of San Leon, Mrs. Bertha Davis of Brazoria, Mrs. Beulah Campbell of Saratoga, Mrs. Jfcttie Chote [Gladys ?] of Houston and Mrs. Clara Linkous, also of Houston. Other survivors are two sisters, Mrs. Mandy Eichelberger of Dayton and Mrs. Ada Lemons of Baytown; a brother, Arthur Shook of Jacinto City, 19 grandchildren and 30 great - grandchildren.

Notes

Beulah's death certificate records Arthur Shook as her father and Molly Davenport as her mother. The informant was Fred Zlomke, and he got about half of that information correct. As stated in the obituary, Arthur Shook was Beulah's brother. Her mother was Molly Furr (not Davenport). The only document linking Beulah to her father is Theodrick's obituary, which sites Mrs. Beulah Zlomke as a surviving daughter. Likewise, the only document linking Beulah to her mother is Molly's obituary, which also sites Mrs. Beulah Zlomke as a surviving daughter.
