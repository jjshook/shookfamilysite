Notes

William Nelson Pate's father, Benagy Pate, owned land near Faceville, Decatur County, Georgia, which is not far from Marianna, Jackson County, Florida.

William Nelson Pate enlisted Co B 5th FL Cav at Quincy, Jefferson County (next to Jackson), Florida on 21-FEB-1863. He transferred to the Kilcrease Artillery with enlistment to back dated to 27-JAN-1863. This unit guarded the narrows of the Chattahoochee River south of Chattahoochee, Florida. He was paroled in Tallahassee 27-JAN-1865 as a member of the Kilcrease Artillery.

On September 27, 1865 William married Mrs. Clarkey Oliver (nee McDaniel) in Jackson County, Florida.

The 1870 Federal Census for Jackson County, Florida records William at 30 years old and a farmer and Clarkie (mispelled) at 30 years old and at home.

About 1874 the family migrated to Montgomery County, Texas.

In the 1890 Montgomery County, Texas Census (compiled by Mary C. Moody), the William Nelson Pate family was recorded as follows:

"PATE, W.N.(1); PATE, W.N., 37, NC, C.L.,37,GA. SN 82C (2); PATE, Wm. N., 60, SC. Ch: Fannie, 26, FL, Tassie, 19, TX, Louis N., 17, TX. SN 63B (3) [Wm N. listed as m - PATE, Clarkie, 60, GA. [m] listed as mo-in-law to NEIDIGH, Fred. SN 63B (3). Can this be the wife of Wm. N.?]; PATE, Wm. N. p. 569 (9)"
