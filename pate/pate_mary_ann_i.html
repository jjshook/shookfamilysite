Obituary (Fred)

Frederic "Fred" V. Hormuth

April 5, 1932 - July 31, 2013

Frederic "Fred" V. Hormuth, 81, of Newburgh entered his heavenly home on Wednesday July 31, 2013. He was born to Viola Cecilia (Weyer) and Marion Vincent Hormuth on April 5, 1932 in Dade City, FL.

Fred graduated from Memorial High School and St. Edward's University in Austin, TX. He married MaryAnn Pate on May 7, 1955, in Refugio, TX. He served in the U.S. Army during the Korean War. He owned and operated Hormuth Drywall and Painting Service, Inc. Fred was a devoted husband, loving father and grandfather. He was formerly a member of Good Shepherd Catholic Church and for the past 45 years has been a very active member of Holy Rosary Catholic Church including singing with the choir and past president of the parish council. Over the years, Fred volunteered and participated in many organizations including; Evansville East Kiwanis President in 1962, Chairman of Evansville Diocesan Education Commission. He was very active with trade organizations and served on The Painting and Decorating Contractors of America - Indiana Council President 1973 - 1974, technical committees for Association of Walls and Ceilings Industries and was a contractor representative on committees for Painters Local 156. Fred was a member of Rolling Hills Country Club where he enjoyed playing golf and cards. Over the years he enjoyed playing cards including couples bridge, his men's bridge club and recently duplicate bridge at the Evansville Duplicate Bridge Club with his daughter Janice and friends. Fred played alto sax with the Newburgh's Old Dam Community Band. He made the July 4th performance in Newburgh and even recently played in the Terre Haute Band Festival on July 20th.

Fred was preceded in death by his parents; brother, William "Billy" Hormuth; grandson, Adam Wheeler; great granddaughter, Madison Treadway; brother-in-law, William "Bill" Haas and nephews, Ronald Wiggers and Mark McCabe.

He is survived by his wife of 58 years, MaryAnn; daughters, Karen Elaine Modesitt (Glenn), Janice Diane Wheeler (Stan) all of Evansville, Linda Marie Hite and Sarah Frances Pillischafske (Robert) both of Louisville, KY; grandsons, Ray Wheeler (Ashley), Paul Hite III, CPT Scott Modesitt, Mark Modesitt, Tom Pillischafske and James Pillischafske; granddaughter, Alisha Hite Treadway; great grandchildren, Andrew Hite, Aubrey Hite and Savannah Treadway; brother, Thomas Hormuth (Barbara) of Rockdale, TX; sisters Mary Ann Wiggers (Carl) of Newnan, GA, Anna Lee Haas of Newburgh, IN and Betty Jo McCabe (Cornelius quot;Mac") of San Antonio, FL; brother-in-law, Andrew Pate, Jr. (Carol)of College Station, TX, uncle, William "Willie" Weyer of Bradenton, FL, several nieces,nephews and cousins.
