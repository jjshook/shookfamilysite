Obituary

Mrs. J. B. Charpiot

Charpiot - Mrs. J. B. Charpiot, 75, of 9114 Humble Road died at 8:00 a. m. on Friday. She had lived in Houston 48 years. Survivors include three sons, Joseph Shook of Mount Bellville, C. D. Shook of Palestine, and A. O. Shook of Baytown; four daughters, Mrs. A. R. Eichelberger of Mount Bellville, Mrs. Ada Etie of Industry, Mrs. Beulah Zlomke of Baytown, and Mrs. Artie Emerson of Beaumont. Two sisters, Bell Clayton and Mrs. Josephine Furr of Upton, 52 grandchildren and 35 great grandchildren. (more not recorded) -- Houston Post, September 17, 1938 (jjshook, transcribed from microfiche at the Houston Public Library, 01-FEB-2014)

Notes

Mollie probably married Theodrick around 1880 in Texas, since Theodoric was born in Harris County, Texas and the Furr family was living in Bastrop, Texas during the 1880 US Census. My sister, Dana, and I searched for Mollie's obituary at the Houston Public Library on February 1, 2014. We found the obituary in both the Houston Chronicle and the Houston Post; however, the text was very difficult to read on the microfiche. The microfiche reader saves the picture to a USB card, but the file was (unfortunatly) corrupted. I'm glad I wrote down most of the text. I was mainly trying to get the surviving names (especially the women's married names).
